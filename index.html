Zip

Transformable Knee Scooter

❮
❯

I helped design and fabricate Zip, a knee scooter that transforms into crutches, as part of my 2.009 project. This was a very difficult challenge with many iterations and design changes before turning into the product it currently is. With the user experience in mind, we designed Zip to be comfortable and easy to use, with a transformation time of under 15 seconds, it is faster than tying your shoes!

  • Date: Dec 2018
  • Location: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • Category: Product Design

PAL

Wearable Device

PAL is a wearable system that uses on-device machine learning to assist users with language learning, memory augmentation, and self awareness. I helped design the hardware for the project, including the ear piece, ring, and base holder. When designing PAL, not only did I focus on the functionality and comfort of the device, but the aesthetics as well, to create a wearable product that people would want to wear.

  • Date: Dec 2018
  • Location: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • Category: Product Design

Wolfram Alpha

Robotics

I designed and created this robot, Wolfram Alpha, as part of my 2.007 class project. For a Willy Wonka themed competition, I chose to tackle one of the hardest tasks of collecting eggs and depositing them in a scale. Not only could this robot suck in and release eggs with its 12 wheels, it can also climb up a 60 degrees hill in order to reach the eggs. In addition, I also used the last alotted motor to create a companion robot, Wolfram Alpha that could complete one of the tasks of the competition by using a single motor to extend a tape measure. While I did not win the compeition, I did win the Whitelaw Prize for originality in design.

  • Date: May 2018
  • Location: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • Category: Robotics

Myomo: Juno

Powered Arm Orthosis

Myomo is a company that designs and builds powered arm orthosis for people who do not have full control of their arms. While they already have adult devices in the market, they are now working on a pediatrics device for children as well. As part of my internship, I helped prototype and test different aspects of the device, including the finger grasp, the forearm strap attachment, the humeral cuff orthosis, and the motorbox shells (as shown above). I designed primarily in Solidworks and used a variety of fabrication and testing tools while at my internship.

  • Date: Aug 2018
  • Location: Myomo
  • Category: Design and Fabrication

Promobot

T-shirt Shooting Robot

As part of my robotics internship with Oak Ridge National Labs, I decided to take on the project of redesigning and rebuilding a t-shirt shooting robot for my robotics team. I started from scratch in Solidworks and designed the whole robot on my own before going on to 3D print all the parts. The abs-infused with carbon fiber robot was printed on a 8ftx8ftx8ft 3D printer. I then added electronics and other peripheral elements such as LED strips and speakers. This robot was showcased at football games and other educational events.

  • Date: July 2015
  • Location: Oak Ridge National Labs
  • Category: Robotics

Intelligent Feed Forward

Simulations For Metal Additive Manufacturing

This was my internship project while at Lawrence Livermore National Labs. Using python and GitHub, I helped develop an intelligent feed forward system to simulate a melt pool from a metal 3D printer. I ran simulations and compared my results to literature in order to justify that the simulated melt pool was accurate. By predicting the melt pool, it can be possible to minimize the amount of defects in a part that might have arose at places such as overhangs and turnarounds.

  • Date: July 2017
  • Location: Lawrence Livermore National Labs
  • Category: Computations

SkinMorph

Design and Fabrication

This is one of the Undergraduate Research project I worked on in my sophomore year. The project was to design and 3D print molds that is used to create a tunable second layer of skin. The tunable skin has a circuitry embedded that hardens the skin when it turns on. During the project, I 3D printed multiple iteration of molds and injection molded them to create a tunable second layer of skin.
