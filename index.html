Promobot

T-shirt Shooting Robot.

As part of my robotics internship with Oak Ridge National Labs, I decided to take on the project of redesigning and rebuilding a t-shirt shooting robot for my robotics team. I started from scratch in Solidworks and designed the whole robot on my own before going on to 3D print all the parts. The abs-infused with carbon fiber robot was printed on a 8ftx8ftx8ft 3D printer. I then added electronics and other peripheral elements such as LED strips and speakers. This robot was showcased at football games and other educational events.

  • Date: July 2015
  • Location: Oak Ridge National Labs
  • Category: Robotics

Speed Snails

Toy Product Design

This toy idea began as a concept of remote controlled bumper cars that I had brainstormed. The RC car soon took on the shape of a snail and the game play began there. The snails have an LED health bar that indicates when they have been hit, as well as shells that can be knocked off once all their health have been lost. Along with my team members, I worked on various aspects of the project from soldering to thermoforming to polishing a 3D printed shell.

  • Date: May 2017
  • Location: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • Category: Toy Design

Check Valve

Design and 3D Printed

This was the project for my first Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP)while at MIT. I designed an experimental setup for testing a 3D printed check valve. I then designed the check valve using SolidWorks and 3D printed the check valve for testing. I went through multiple design iterations in order to get the check valve to function as effectively as possible.

  • Date: May 2017
  • Location: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • Category: Design

Intelligent Feed Forward

Simulations For Metal Additive Manufacturing

This was my internship project while at Lawrence Livermore National Labs. Using python and GitHub, I helped develop an intelligent feed forward system to simulate a melt pool from a metal 3D printer. I ran simulations and compared my results to literature in order to justify that the simulated melt pool was accurate. By predicting the melt pool, it can be possible to minimize the amount of defects in a part that might have arose at places such as overhangs and turnarounds.

  • Date: July 2017
  • Location: Lawrence Livermore National Labs
  • Category: Computations

Grand Piano

Designed and 3D Printed

This piano was designed using AutoDesk Inventor. The pieces were 3D printed and assembled, leaving a grand piano with a cover that can open and close. It was designed to house an arduino, which plays music when a button is clicked.

  • Date: May 2014
  • Location: Oak Ridge National Labs
  • Category: Design

SkinMorph

Design and Fabrication

This is the Undergraduate Research I am currently working on. The project is to design and 3D print molds that will be used to create a tunable second layer of skin. The tunable skin will have circuitry that can for example harden the skin. Once the molds have been 3D printed, I will use injection molding to create the tunable second layer of skin.

  • Date: September 2017
  • Location: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • Category: Design
